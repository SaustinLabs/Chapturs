# Chapturs Marketing Strategy

## LLM Prompts for Tweet Generation

Copy-paste these prompts into ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini. Fill in `[PLACEHOLDERS]` before submitting.

---

### 1. Featured Story Tease

```
Write 3 short punchy tweet options (under 240 chars each) to promote a webnovel called "[STORY TITLE]" by @[AUTHOR_HANDLE].

Genre: [GENRE]
One-line premise: [PREMISE]
Mood: [e.g. "dark, philosophical" / "wholesome found family" / "chaotic action"]

Rules:
- Hook in the first 5 words
- No spoilers
- End with: "Read free on Chapturs → chapturs.com/story/[STORY_ID]"
- Avoid generic phrases like "you won't believe" or "must read"

Generate 3 variants: one mysterious, one emotional, one action-driven.
```

---

### 2. Creator Spotlight

```
Write a short Twitter thread (3 tweets, under 240 chars each) spotlighting a creator on the Chapturs platform.

Creator name: [NAME or @HANDLE]
Genre they write: [GENRE]
Their best-performing story: [TITLE]
One interesting fact about them: [FACT]

Format:
Tweet 1: Hook / intriguing fact about the creator
Tweet 2: About their work and why readers love it
Tweet 3: CTA — follow them on Chapturs + direct link

Tone: warm, genuine, community-driven. Never sycophantic.
```

---

### 3. Platform Value Prop (Awareness)

```
Write 5 tweet options that explain why Chapturs is different from platforms like Wattpad, Webnovel, and RoyalRoad.

Key differentiators:
- 70% ad revenue goes directly to creators (vs 0-30% elsewhere)
- Free to read — no paywalls ever
- Authors keep 100% of their IP
- Open Beta, actively being built with community input

Rules:
- Max 240 chars per tweet
- No corporate-speak
- Speak directly to: (a) readers who want free stories, (b) writers who've been burned by other platforms
- Include "chapturs.com" in at least 3 of the 5

Generate both reader-focused and creator-focused variants.
```

---

### 4. Writing Tip (Top-of-Funnel)

```
Write 3 writing tip tweets for webnovel authors. Topic: [TOPIC e.g. "chapter pacing" / "writing villains" / "building a magic system"].

Rules:
- Max 240 chars
- Practical, actionable, specific
- End one of them with a soft CTA: "Publish your writing on Chapturs → chapturs.com"
- Tone: peer-to-peer, not teacher-to-student

Format each as a standalone tweet (not a thread).
```

---

### 5. Contest Announcement

Write a tweet thread (4 tweets) announcing a writing contest on Chapturs.

Tweet 1: Hype/attention hook

Max 240 chars per tweet. Tone: exciting but not spammy.
```

Write 2 variants:

```

### 7. Engagement / Community Hook

```
Write 5 engagement tweet options for a webnovel platform community.

Context: These are for @chapturs, a platform for webnovel readers and writers.

Types to include:
- 1x "This or That" style poll prompt (e.g. "Fantasy or Sci-Fi?")

Max 240 chars each. Fun, community-building tone.
```

## Growth Tactics

### Short-term (Beta)
1. **Engage in webnovel/writing communities** — Reply to #WebNovel, #WritingCommunity, #AmWriting tags daily (not just promotion, genuine replies)
2. **Retweet and quote-tweet creators** on the platform — turn them into advocates
3. **Author threads** — "How I wrote 100k words in 3 months" style content that naturally plugs Chapturs
4. **Tag-along with trending writing topics** — NaNoWriMo, BookTok trends, etc.

### Medium-term
1. **Partner with small-to-mid WebNovel YouTubers/TikTokers** for platform reviews
2. **Run a "Founding Creator" campaign** — first 100 creators get a badge + special perks
3. **Contest virality loop** — Contests bring in readers who share results

### SEO / Content
- Start a creator-focused blog: "Webnovel Writing Tips", "Platform Comparison: Chapturs vs RoyalRoad"
- These posts also fuel tweet content through the LLM prompts above

---

## Voice & Tone Guide

- **Not:** "We're excited to announce..." / "Our revolutionary platform..."
- **Yes:** "We shipped [X]. Here's what it means for you as a creator."
- Direct, honest, occasionally self-deprecating about being in Beta
- Always center the creator or the reader — not the platform itself
- Celebrate the community loudly; criticise competitors rarely and gently
